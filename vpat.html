Accessibility Conformance Report (VPAT®)

PollSlide Technologies LLC · Based on VPAT® 2.5 · Report date:

🌐 This page has been translated for convenience. The English version is the authoritative one.

This Accessibility Conformance Report (ACR) follows the Voluntary Product Accessibility Template® (VPAT®) version 2.5 published by the Information Technology Industry Council (ITI). It documents how PollSlide conforms to WCAG 2.1 Level A and AA, the EU harmonised standard EN 301 549 (V3.2.1), and Revised Section 508 of the U.S. Rehabilitation Act. It is intended for procurement teams at schools, universities, government bodies and enterprises. For a plain-language overview, see our Accessibility Statement.

Product information

  • Name & version: PollSlide web application (app.pollslide.com), pollslide.com marketing site, and PollSlide Companion for macOS — as of the report date.
  • Product description: Live audience-response software (polls, quizzes, surveys and study sessions). Presenters build and run interactive decks; audiences join from any device by QR code or short code at pollslide.com/join and answer at app.pollslide.com/answer.
  • Evaluation methods: Self-assessment combining manual keyboard-only testing, screen-reader spot checks (VoiceOver on macOS/iOS, NVDA on Windows), automated tooling (axe-core, Lighthouse), colour-contrast analysis, and 200% zoom / reflow review.
  • Contact: help@pollslide.com

Conformance terms

  • Supports — the functionality meets the criterion without known defects.
  • Partially Supports — some functionality meets the criterion; some does not.
  • Does Not Support — the majority of functionality does not meet the criterion.
  • Not Applicable — the criterion does not apply to the product.

Overall self-assessed level: Partially Supports WCAG 2.1 AA. The participant answer flow — the surface used by the widest and most diverse audience — is prioritised and closest to full conformance. Known gaps and target dates are listed at the end of this report.

Known limitations & remediation roadmap

  • Presenter workspace ARIA: some drag-to-arrange and slide-editing controls in the presenter workspace have partial screen-reader coverage. Target: rolling improvements each release.
  • Secondary text contrast: a small set of muted/secondary labels are being raised to 4.5:1. Target: next accessibility pass.
  • Live-region announcements: extending programmatic announcements for live result updates. In progress.
  • Host-authored content: the accessibility of presenter-chosen images, colours and embedded media depends on the host; we provide guidance but cannot guarantee third-party content.
